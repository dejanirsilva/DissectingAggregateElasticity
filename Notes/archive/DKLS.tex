  We study the aggregate stock market elasticity in a general equilibrium model with heterogeneous investors, passive demand, and financial constraints. Using state-global perturbations, we show that general equilibrium adjustments of the risk-free rate and risk premium govern flow-driven price dynamics. With nonzero cross-price elasticity and efficient risk allocation, markets are infinitely elastic even if individual investors are inelastic. When risk is misallocated and flows shift the consumption-wealth ratio, flows raise prices, with larger effects when active wealth share is low. Heterogeneity can raise elasticity and leverage constraints amplify price impact. A quantitative implementation confirms these mechanisms and explains excess market volatility.
